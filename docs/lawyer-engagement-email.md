# Lawyer engagement email — ready to send

Copy the body below into a fresh email to your chosen Kenyan commercial
lawyer (one with **MLM / consumer-protection / data-protection** experience).
Attach `docs/legal-review-packet.md` as a PDF; the markdown reads cleanly
when exported via your editor or a converter like `pandoc`.

The packet is structured so the lawyer can read it in 30–45 minutes and form
an opinion; the email below gives them three clearly-scoped engagement
options so they can quote you immediately rather than scoping back-and-forth.

---

## Subject

Loveli Luxury Scents — pre-launch legal review (compensation plan + partner program)

## Body

Hello [Advocate's first name],

I'm Ashish, founder of Loveli Luxury Scents — a Kenya-based premium Eau de
Parfum e-commerce store with an invite-only partner program. We're about
two weeks from a sandboxed soft-launch and would like a Kenyan lawyer's
opinion on whether the program, compensation plan, and customer-facing
terms are defensible under Kenyan law before we take real money.

Attached is a single-document review packet
(`legal-review-packet.md` — converted to PDF) covering:

- What Loveli is and how the two registers (retail + partner) are kept
  separated in product
- The compensation plan (5 ranks, retail-only commissions, no recruitment
  pay, capped levels)
- The partner T&Cs, income disclosure statement, refund + clawback model
- The Kenyan laws we believe apply (Companies Act 2015 §502, POCAMLA,
  Data Protection Act 2019, Consumer Protection Act 2012, Sale of Goods Act)
- The six specific questions we'd like you to answer

The packet is designed to be read in 30–45 minutes. If anything is missing
or unclear, please reply and I'll have a follow-up document the same day.

### Engagement options

To save us both a scoping call, here are three clearly-bounded options.
Please reply with the option you're prepared to take on, your fee, and
your earliest available turnaround. We can sign whatever engagement
letter you prefer.

1. **Review-only opinion (≈ 4–6 hours).** You read the packet, answer
   the six questions in writing, flag anything materially wrong, and
   identify the top three risks. No redrafting. Best if the program is
   broadly compliant and you just need to confirm.

2. **Red-line of the T&Cs + IDS (≈ 8–12 hours).** Includes the
   review-only deliverable plus specific edits to the customer-facing
   partner T&Cs, Income Disclosure Statement, and refund/clawback
   language. We get a marked-up document back that we can ship.

3. **Oral opinion over 45 minutes (≈ 1.5 hours).** You read the packet,
   we get on a call, and you walk us through the top issues. No written
   product. Best if we need to make a launch decision fast and a
   follow-up engagement can happen later.

### Target turnaround

We're hoping for the deliverable within **five working days** of
engagement. If that's not realistic for you, please tell us your honest
earliest date — we'd rather wait two more weeks for the right lawyer
than rush a substandard review.

### What we are NOT asking for

- A pyramid-scheme legal opinion *in absolute terms* — your scope is
  whether the program as documented in the packet is on the right side
  of Kenyan law, not a general defence of MLM.
- Trademark or IP work — separate workstream.
- Drafting the master services agreement — out of scope here.

If you'd like a 15-minute discovery call before quoting, I'm reachable on
WhatsApp at [your number] or this email.

Best regards,
Ashish
Founder, Loveli Luxury Scents
[your phone] · [website]

## How to send

1. Attach the packet as PDF. From `loveli-luxury/`:
   `pandoc docs/legal-review-packet.md -o legal-review-packet.pdf`
   (or open the markdown in any editor with PDF export).
2. Add one lawyer to To: and CC `ruth@…` so the client/admin is in the loop.
3. **Do not** mass-send. A single named recipient gets a 70%+ reply rate;
   three at once gets none.
4. Set a calendar reminder for 5 working days; if no reply, send a short
   nudge ("checking in — happy to widen the scope or shorten the timeline").

## Who to send to (suggested filters)

Look for a Kenyan advocate whose Law Society of Kenya profile or firm
website mentions any of:

- Multi-level marketing / direct sales / network marketing
- Consumer protection or competition law
- Data Protection Act 2019 advisory
- E-commerce / fintech regulatory work

Avoid pure-litigation or pure-conveyancing firms — they will accept the
brief but produce a brittle review.
